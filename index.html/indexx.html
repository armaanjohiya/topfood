    1. prepare the sushi rice

      • Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear. This removes excess starch.
      • In a medium saucepan, combine the rinsed rice and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 18-20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and water is absorbed.
      • In a small bowl, mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt until dissolved. Once the rice is cooked, remove it from heat and gently fold in the vinegar mixture. Allow the rice to cool to room temperature.

    2. preparing the filling

      • Slice the avocado, cucumber, carrot, and bell pepper into thin strips.
      • If using tofu or tempeh, pan-fry or grill until golden and seasoned to your liking.

    3. Assemble the sushi

      • Place a bamboo sushi mat on a flat surface. Lay a sheet of nori (seaweed) on the mat, shiny side down.
      • Wet your hands to prevent sticking, and take a handful of sushi rice. Spread it evenly over the nori, leaving a small border at the top.
      • Arrange your desired fillings in a line across the center of the rice.
    4. Roll the sushi

      • Starting from the bottom, use the bamboo mat to lift the edge of the mat and roll it over the fillings, pressing gently but firmly.

    Ingredients

    For the Vegetable Mix

    • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, bell peppers, beans, etc.)
    • 1 cup diced tomatoes
    • 1 medium onion, sliced
    • 2 tablespoons garlic paste
    • 2 tablespoons ginger paste
    • 1-2 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil (or any cooking oil)
    • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1 tablespoon garam masala
    • 1 tablespoon Italian seasoning (or mixed dried herbs)
    • Salt to taste
    • Fresh basil or cilantro, for garnish

      • for the rice

      • 2 cups basmati rice
      • 4 cups water
      • 1 bay leaf
      • 3-4 green cardamom pods
      • 3-4 cloves
      • 1 stick of cinnamon
      • Salt to taste

        • instructions

        1. prepare the rice

          • Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak for 30 minutes, then drain.
          • In a pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, and salt. Add the soaked rice and cook until it’s about 70% done. Drain and set aside.
        2. cook the vegetables

          • In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle.
          • Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown. Then add garlic and ginger paste, followed by the green chilies. Cook for a few minutes until fragrant.
          • Add mixed vegetables and diced tomatoes. Sprinkle turmeric, garam masala, Italian seasoning, and salt. Stir well and cook until the vegetables are tender (about 5-7 minutes).
        3. Layer the Biryani:

          • In the pot with the vegetable mixture, spread half of the partially cooked rice evenly over the veggies.
          • Sprinkle half of the grated cheese and some fresh herbs.
          • Add the remaining rice on top, followed by the rest of the cheese.
        4. final cooking

          • Cover the pot tightly with a lid (you can seal it with a damp cloth and then place the lid on top to trap the steam). Cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and the rice to finish cooking.
        5. serve

          • Gently fluff the biryani with a fork. Garnish with fried onions, additional herbs, and serve hot with raita or salad.
        6. TIPS

          • Feel free to add your favorite vegetables or even tofu for added protein.
          • Adjust the spices according to your taste preference.

      • 3 Butter Chicken

        • to make this you have to follow these step

        ingredients

        for the chicken marinade

        • 500g boneless chicken (cut into bite-sized pieces)
        • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
        • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
        • 1 tbsp lemon juice
        • 1 tsp red chili powder
        • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
        • 1 tsp garam masala
        • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
        • Salt to taste

        for the gravy sauce

        • tbsp butter (unsalted)
        • 1 tbsp oil (vegetable or canola)
        • 1 large onion, finely chopped
        • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
        • 2 large tomatoes, pureed (or use canned tomato puree)
        • 1 tsp red chili powder
        • 1 tsp garam masala
        • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
        • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
        • 1/2 cup heavy cream
        • 1/2 cup water (adjust to the consistency you prefer)
        • 1 tbsp sugar (optional, but adds balance)
        • Salt to taste
        • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

        instructions

        step 1: marinate the chicken

        1. Combine the marinade ingredients: In a bowl, mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, cumin powder, and salt.
        2. Coat the chicken: Add the chicken pieces to the marinade and coat them well. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to an hour (preferably in the fridge). If you have more time, marinating overnight will enhance the flavor.

        step 2: cook the chicken

        1. Grill Method: Preheat a grill or grill pan and cook the marinated chicken until it's cooked through and slightly charred (about 8–10 minutes).
        2. Pan-Fry Method: Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan over medium-high heat and cook the marinated chicken until browned and cooked through (about 8–10 minutes). Set aside.

        step 3: prepare the gravy

        1. Heat butter and oil: In a large pan or skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of butter and 1 tablespoon of oil over medium heat.
        2. Cook onions: Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown, around 5-7 minutes.
        3. Add ginger-garlic paste: Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 2 minutes until fragrant.
        4. Tomato puree: Add the pureed tomatoes and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the oil starts to separate from the tomato mixture.
        5. Spices: Add red chili powder, garam masala, cumin powder, and coriander powder. Stir well and cook for another 2 minutes.
        6. Add cream and sugar: Lower the heat and add the heavy cream and sugar. Stir well, letting it simmer for a few minutes until the gravy becomes smooth and creamy.
        7. Add water: Adjust the consistency by adding water, depending on how thick you want the gravy. Let it simmer for 5–10 minutes.

        step 4: Combine Chicken with Gravy

        1. Add the cooked chicken: Add the grilled or pan-fried chicken pieces to the gravy. Stir gently to coat the chicken with the sauce.
        2. Simmer: Let the chicken simmer in the gravy for about 5–10 minutes, so it absorbs the flavors of the sauce.
        3. Final touch: Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of butter and stir until it melts into the sauce. This will give the curry its signature richness and shine.

        step 5: Garnish and Serve

        1. Garnish: Sprinkle chopped fresh cilantro on top of the butter chicken.
        2. Serve: Serve the butter chicken hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice. You can also serve it with a side of cucumber raita or a simple salad.

        Tips for the Perfect Butter Chicken:

        • Yogurt in the marinade: Yogurt tenderizes the chicken and helps in infusing flavors. Make sure you use plain, unsweetened yogurt.

        • Smoky flavor: To add a smoky flavor (like the one in restaurant-style butter chicken), you can use the "dhungar" method. Heat a piece of charcoal until it’s red hot, place it in a small bowl, and pour a teaspoon of ghee or oil over it. Cover the pan immediately to trap the smoke and let it infuse for 5–10 minutes.

        • Butter: Use plenty of butter for richness. It’s a key ingredient that gives the dish its signature smooth, velvety texture.

        • Adjust the spice level: If you prefer a milder butter chicken, reduce the amount of chili powder and use less garam masala.

        Enjoy this delicious, indulgent dish with your favorite sides!
